Data-Driven Visual Effects

What we can render in Three.js from the actual exoplanet data fields

Rocky / Terrestrial
ρ > 4 g/cm³ · R < 1.5 R⊕
✦Cratered, dusty surface texture
✦Red/brown/grey tones from density
✦Thin or no atmosphere glow
✦Ice caps if eq. temp < 200K
✦Glowing lava cracks if temp > 1000K
Ocean / Water World
R 1.5–2.5 R⊕ · low density · ~300K
✦Deep blue ocean with specular shine
✦Swirling cloud layer (semi-transparent)
✦Blue atmospheric rim glow
✦Polar ice caps from eq. temp
✦Scattered landmass if density is higher
Super-Earth / Habitable
R 1–2 R⊕ · insol ~0.25–1.5 F⊕
✦Green/blue mixed surface (land + ocean)
✦Thin atmosphere glow (blue-white)
✦Cloud layer density from insolation flux
✦"Habitable zone" badge from flux range
✦Ice/desert shift based on temp
Sub-Neptune / Ice Giant
R 2–4 R⊕ · low density
✦Blue-purple hazy atmosphere
✦Subtle banding in cloud layer
✦Strong rim glow (thick atmosphere)
✦Rings if radius is on the larger end
Gas Giant
R > 4 R⊕ · M > 50 M⊕ · low density
✦Animated horizontal cloud bands
✦Great spot storm (procedural)
✦Ring system (optional, data-driven)
✦Color shifts warm→cool by eq. temp
Hot Jupiter
R > 8 R⊕ · temp > 1000K · high insol
✦Glowing orange-red surface
✦Atmospheric ablation on star-facing side
✦Intense rim glow (heated atmosphere)
✦Tidal-lock day/night terminator line
Universal Effects (applied to all types)
✦ Star-direction lighting from host star type
